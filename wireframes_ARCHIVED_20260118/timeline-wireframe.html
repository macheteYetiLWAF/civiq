Timeline Feature - Design Notes

Scope Toggle

  • National - Full U.S. history from pre-colonial (1400s) to present
  • State (Pennsylvania) - PA-specific events: William Penn, state constitution, industrial history, coal mining, steel, etc.

Era Quick Navigation

  • Pre-Colonial (1400-1607): Indigenous peoples, European exploration
  • Colonial (1607-1765): Jamestown, Plymouth, 13 colonies form
  • Revolution (1765-1783): Stamp Act through Treaty of Paris
  • Founding (1783-1800): Constitution, Bill of Rights, early republic
  • Expansion (1800-1860): Louisiana Purchase, westward movement, industrialization
  • Civil War (1860-1877): Secession, war, Reconstruction
  • Gilded Age (1877-1900): Industrialization, labor movements, immigration
  • Progressive (1900-1920): Reform, WWI, women's suffrage
  • World Wars (1920-1945): Depression, WWII
  • Modern (1945-Present): Cold War, Civil Rights, digital age

Event Categories

  • Legislation - Major bills, acts of Congress
  • Courts - Supreme Court rulings, landmark cases
  • Amendments - Constitutional amendments (all 27)
  • Political - Elections, assassinations, uprisings, scandals
  • Economic - Industries, recessions, trade policies
  • Inventions - Technology, innovations that shaped economy
  • Disasters - Natural disasters, major accidents
  • Settlement - Exploration, colonization, notable settlers/leaders

Pennsylvania State Timeline Examples

  • 1681 - William Penn receives charter from Charles II
  • 1682 - Penn founds Philadelphia
  • 1701 - Charter of Privileges (PA's first constitution)
  • 1776 - PA Constitution (most democratic of its time)
  • 1787 - Constitutional Convention in Philadelphia
  • 1859 - First commercial oil well (Titusville)
  • 1889 - Johnstown Flood (2,209 deaths)
  • 1892 - Homestead Strike
  • 1979 - Three Mile Island nuclear accident
